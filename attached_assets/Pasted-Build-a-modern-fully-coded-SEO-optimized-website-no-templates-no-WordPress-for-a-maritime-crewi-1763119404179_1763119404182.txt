Build a modern, fully coded, SEO-optimized website (no templates, no WordPress) for a maritime crewing company called “AURA SEA”.

GENERAL BRAND & TONE
- Brand name: AURA SEA
- Tagline: “CREWING WITH CARE”
- Slogan for ship-owners: “Reliable, long-term crewing partner for tanker and bulk fleets.”
- Slogan for seafarers: “Transparent contracts, timely payments, and real career growth.”
- Concept: “Fresh Vision” – modern, startup-like, technology-driven approach to crewing and crew management.
- Tone of voice: professional, trustworthy, human, caring and future-oriented. No clichés, no generic “lorem ipsum”. Generate real, specific copy.

OVERALL GOAL
The website is a VISIT CARD with excellent SEO and strong design.  
No CV upload or complex backend for now. It must clearly speak to:
1) Ship-owners (primary target – decision makers who need a reliable crewing partner).
2) Seafarers (officers and ratings who may later apply through vacancies).

TECH / IMPLEMENTATION
- Create a responsive, production-ready site using standard HTML, CSS and JavaScript (no heavy frameworks). Organize code cleanly in separate files: index.html (+ other pages), styles.css, script.js.
- The site must be easy to deploy on a Hostinger VPS with Nginx as static files.
- Implement smooth but lightweight animations in CSS/JS (no huge libraries).
- Include comments in the code for key parts (structure, animations, analytics placeholders, how to later connect forms to Google Sheets).

DESIGN & STYLE
- Overall style: modern, “Fresh Vision”, using strong glassmorphism (large frosted glass panels with blur, transparency and light borders).
- Background: light, maritime gradient inspired by sea water and sky horizon; for example a gradient from soft light blue at the top to slightly deeper blue/turquoise at the bottom. It should look like calm water meeting the sky.
- Use a large gradient circle (blue-violet to blue-cyan) in the hero or behind the main glass card, similar to the provided AURA SEA visuals.
- Place content inside glassmorphism “cards” or “stickers” – they should look like semi-transparent fogged glass floating above the water gradient.
- Add subtle animated “little ships” (minimalistic ship icons) in the hero area that gently float or move slightly as a background animation. Keep it tasteful and not distracting.
- Use rounded corners, soft shadows, and smooth hover states on buttons.
- Colors:
  - Main: variations of blue (navy, royal blue, cyan) and white.
  - Accent: gradient blue-violet / blue-pink, used in the hero circle, CTAs and key elements.
  - Background: light blue water/sky gradient as described.
- Typography:
  - Use a strong, clean sans-serif headline font similar in feel to the logo (bold, uppercase for headlines).
  - Body text should be very readable, medium weight.
  - Use consistent hierarchy: H1 hero, H2 section titles, H3 small subtitles.

ANIMATIONS & INTERACTION
- Animate the hero gradient circle very subtly (slow movement or glow).
- Animate the small ships (floating / slight horizontal drift).
- On hover, CTA buttons should slightly grow and get a soft glow or gradient border.
- When scrolling, sections can fade/slide in lightly (e.g., using intersection observers in vanilla JS).

SITE STRUCTURE & PAGES
Create the following pages (each with its own HTML file, proper <title> and <meta> description):

1) HOME
- Hero section:
  - Logo placeholder at the top (assume AURA SEA logo will be provided as an image).
  - Big tagline: “CREWING WITH CARE”.
  - Short explanatory sentence for ship-owners: “Reliable, long-term crewing partner for tanker and bulk fleets.”
  - Two primary CTAs:
    - “For Ship-Owners” (links to that page).
    - “For Seafarers” (links to that page).
  - Background: the described light water/horizon gradient, big glass card in the center, animated gradient circle behind it, floating ship icons.

- “Fresh Vision” section:
  - Explain the “Fresh Vision” concept: combining maritime expertise (10+ years) with modern processes, transparent communication, and technology.
  - Use 3 glass cards for sub-points (e.g. “People-First Crewing”, “Tech-Enabled Processes”, “Long-Term Partnerships”).

- “For Ship-Owners – Key Benefits” section (summary, linking deeper page):
  - Highlight the three most important points:
    1. Compliance & certifications.
    2. 24/7 communication and support.
    3. Experienced database of officers and crew (mention “Database of 500+ seafarers”).
  - Use icons and short, strong bullet-style explanations.

- “For Seafarers – Why AURA SEA?” section (summary, linking deeper page):
  - Emphasize:
    - Fair contracts.
    - Timely payment.
    - Career support & training opportunities.
  - Keep the tone supportive and respectful; show that AURA SEA is on their side.

- “Experience & Numbers” section:
  - Visual blocks or counters for:
    - “10+ years maritime experience”
    - “500+ seafarers in our database”
    - “Tanker & bulk fleets specialization”
  - Place them in glass cards with subtle animation on scroll.

- Call-to-Action strip:
  - Short line inviting ship-owners to get in touch and a button “Contact AURA SEA”.

2) ABOUT COMPANY
- Tell the story of AURA SEA: maritime roots, crewing expertise, and the Fresh Vision direction.
- Include:
  - Mission statement (crewing with care, long-term partnerships).
  - Vision (building a growing holding company, continuous development, innovation).
  - Values (safety, transparency, respect, reliability, development).
- Include a simple timeline or vertical steps showing the evolution and future plans.
- Add a small “Leadership / Team” block (even if with placeholder names and roles) to humanize the brand.

3) FOR SHIP-OWNERS
- Start with a headline focused on their problems and AURA SEA’s solution.
- Sections:
  - “What We Offer” – crew management for tankers and bulk carriers, full cycle of selection, vetting, documentation, and support.
  - “Compliance & Safety” – mention compliance with international maritime standards, vetting, medical checks, certifications (use realistic but generic wording that can be edited later).
  - “Process Overview” – 3-5 steps (Requirements → Shortlist → Vetting → Deployment → Ongoing support).
  - “Why AURA SEA as your partner” – reiterate reliability, long-term cooperation, 24/7 communication, and the large seafarer pool.
- End with a strong CTA box: “Let’s discuss your crewing needs” with a link to the Contact page.

4) FOR SEAFARERS
- Friendly intro welcoming officers and ratings.
- Sections:
  - “What we offer to you” – fair contracts, timely payment, safe and reputable fleets, long-term cooperation.
  - “Your Career Path” – explain how AURA SEA supports progression, training, and repeated contracts.
  - “How the process works” – simple steps for applying when vacancies appear (register interest, interview, documents, joining vessel).
- Include a highlighted note: currently AURA SEA website is mainly a business card, but vacancies will be regularly posted on the Vacancies page.

5) VACANCIES
- For now this is primarily a placeholder page, but still fully designed.
- Add a short intro text: “Here we will publish active vacancies (e.g. for tanker and bulk fleets).”
- Include 2–3 example vacancy cards (e.g. “Chief Officer – Oil/Chemical Tanker”, “Second Engineer – Bulk Carrier”) with realistic but obviously sample details.
- Each vacancy card should have:
  - Position, vessel type, brief description, example salary range, contract length.
  - A button “Apply for this vacancy”.
- When the user clicks “Apply for this vacancy”, show a small popup glass form or scroll to a form at the bottom with fields:
  - Full name (required)
  - Rank applying for (pre-filled from vacancy but editable)
  - Email (required)
  - Phone / messaging contact (optional)
  - Nationality (optional)
  - Years of experience in rank (optional)
  - Message / link to online CV (optional; NO file upload)
  - GDPR consent checkbox with short text
- For now, the form should not actually send to a backend. Implement JavaScript that:
  - Prevents real submission.
  - Logs the form data to the console.
  - Shows a confirmation message on the page: “Thank you for your submission” (as requested).
  - In comments, explain how to later connect this to a Google Apps Script / Google Sheets endpoint.

6) CONTACTS
- Simple but professional contact page with:
  - Short text inviting both ship-owners and seafarers to get in touch.
  - Contact form (same submission behavior as above – console log + on-page success message “Thank you for your submission”):
    - Name, Email (required), Subject, Message, optional phone, GDPR checkbox.
  - Company contact details section:
    - Placeholder for company legal name, address, email, phone, and working hours.
- Include a static map image placeholder or simple map frame area (no need for real API integration).

7) PRIVACY POLICY / GDPR
- Generate a generic but reasonably detailed privacy policy adapted to a crewing agency collecting contact details and vacancy applications.
- Mention use of contact forms, potential future connection to Google Sheets or other systems, cookies and analytics.
- This does NOT have to be perfect legal text, but should look realistic and structured with headings.

NAVIGATION & FOOTER
- Global header on all pages:
  - Left: logo and “AURA SEA”.
  - Right: navigation links: Home, About, For Ship-Owners, For Seafarers, Vacancies, Contacts.
  - A small “Contact us” CTA button in the header.
- Footer on all pages:
  - Company name, placeholder address, email, phone.
  - Links to Privacy Policy / GDPR.
  - Short line about “10+ years maritime experience” and specialization in tanker & bulk fleets.
  - Social media icon placeholders.

SEO & ANALYTICS
- Implement strong on-page SEO:
  - Unique <title> and <meta name="description"> for each page, using keywords like “crewing agency”, “ship crew management”, “tanker and bulk fleets”, “AURA SEA”, etc.
  - Semantic HTML structure with proper headings (H1, H2, etc.) and descriptive alt text for images (especially anything with ships or crews).
- Add JSON-LD structured data:
  - Organization schema for AURA SEA (with placeholder address, sameAs for future social links).
  - On Vacancies page, include a sample JobPosting schema for one vacancy as an example.
- Add a Google Analytics placeholder:
  - Insert a GA4 script template in the <head> with a placeholder like G-XXXXXXXXXX and a clear comment that the user should replace it with their own ID.

FINAL NOTES
- The site must look visually impressive and modern on both desktop and mobile.
- Prioritize clear messaging for ship-owners while still making seafarers feel welcomed and supported.
- Avoid lorem ipsum; generate all text content according to these guidelines.
- Keep the code clean, commented and easy to extend later (for adding real vacancy backend, actual CV processing and more automation).
